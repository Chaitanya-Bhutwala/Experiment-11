Experiment 11: One-Dimensional Heat Conduction using Finite Difference Method
Overview:

This experiment focuses on solving the one-dimensional heat conduction problem in a rod using numerical methods in MATLAB. The objective is to analyze how temperature varies along the rod over time until steady state is achieved.

Methodology:
Governing heat conduction equation solved using explicit finite difference method
Rod of length 0.75 m discretized into small spatial intervals
Time domain divided into small steps for stable computation
Boundary conditions:
Left end fixed at 100°C
Right end fixed at 0°C
Initial condition: entire rod at 0°C
Iterative update of temperature at interior nodes using finite difference formula
Computation continued until steady state condition is reached

Results:
Temperature distribution along the rod obtained successfully
Observed gradual decrease in temperature from 100°C to 0°C
Final steady-state profile approached a linear distribution
Demonstrated effectiveness of numerical methods in solving heat transfer problems
Provided clear understanding of temperature variation in solid materials
